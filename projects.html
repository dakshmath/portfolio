Breakout

“Breakout” is a Unity game using C#, where every decision shapes the player’s escape. Players navigate a high-security prison, solving puzzles and avoiding guards while facing ethical dilemmas that influence multiple possible endings. The game combines strategy, suspense, and moral choices to challenge players on both skill and ethics.

Published Co-Author - Research Paper on SSRN

Co-authored "The Price of Precision: A Model of Optimal Bias on the Fairness-Accuracy Frontier," this economics paper uses Holmstrom's Informativeness Principle and Bayesian updating models to explain why AI algorithms remain biased despite firms having debiasing capabilities. We demonstrate through formal economic modeling that profit-maximizing firms rationally choose biased algorithms because they preserve more informative signals than fair alternatives. Presented to UPenn Faculty.

NASA Autonomous Rover

Designed an autonomous rover using reinforcement learning (Q-Learning + CMAC) to naviate an open terrain. Initially, we developed a simulation to model the open terrain. Then, we took it to the TurtleBot3 rover,leveraging ROS for simulation, with Python scripts for control and navigation. The rover successfully navigates to a target using 360° LiDAR sensors and raycasting to avoid obstacles in Mars.

Site Selector Device & Coaxial Plugs

At Infinite Biomedical Devices LLC, I created an arrow indicator for a site selector device in SolidWorks that attaches to electrodes. The arrow indicator was designed to help patients identify the correct direction for attaching the electrodes to the muscle belly for accurate strength measurements. Additionally, I also created 4-band & 6-band coax covers for coaxial plugs in SolidWorks in order to prevent any signal/electrical loss and ensure that the control signals and power are transmitted effectively. The covers reduce the risk of interruptions or degradation in the signals, which is crucial for the functionality of the prosthetic hand.

HandTalk

Hand Talk is an innovative device that translates sign language into text in real-time, making communication seamless between hearing-impaired individuals and those unfamiliar with sign language. Using a Raspberry Pi connected to a 5-megapixel camera, it captures gestures frame by frame and converts them into sentences. The translated text is then displayed instantly on an OLED screen for easy reading.
